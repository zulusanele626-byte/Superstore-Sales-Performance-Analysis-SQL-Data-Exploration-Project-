# Superstore-Sales-Performance-Analysis-SQL-Data-Exploration-Project-

The aim of this project is to analyze retail sales data to uncover business insights related to sales performance, customer behavior, product trends, and operational efficiency using SQL.

## Project Objectives

To evaluate overall sales performance across time (years & months)
To identify top-performing regions, states, and products
To analyze customer purchasing behavior and repeat orders
To measure customer value (lifetime value)
To assess shipping efficiency and delivery performance
To break down sales by segment, category, and sub-category

# Process Followed

1. Data Exploration
Previewed dataset using SQL queries
Identified key columns for analysis

3. Data Cleaning
Checked for missing values
Removed duplicates
Converted date columns for accurate calculations

5. Data Analysis
Performed aggregations (SUM, AVG, COUNT)
Analyzed trends by year and month
Evaluated regional and product performance

7. Customer Analysis
Identified repeat customers
Calculated customer lifetime value
Ranked top customers by region

9. Operational Analysis
Measured shipping time using date differences
Compared shipping modes efficiency

## project insight 
Sales show clear trends over time, highlighting periods of growth and decline
A few regions contribute significantly to overall revenue
High-value customers play a major role in total sales
Certain product categories outperform others consistently
Top-selling products generate a large share of total revenue
Repeat customers are critical for sustained business growth
Shipping efficiency varies by shipping mode and impacts delivery time

# Conclusion 

This analysis highlights the importance of focusing on top-performing regions, products, and high-value customers to drive revenue growth. Improving customer retention and optimizing shipping operations can further enhance overall business performance.
The project demonstrates how SQL can be effectively used to extract, analyze, and interpret data to support strategic decision-making.
